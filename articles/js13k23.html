Day 0 (August 13th)

Tough theme indeed, none of the vague ideas I had before starting the jam could fit in or include something about the 13th century.

So I started from scratch, by looking on Wikipedia what was invented and discovered in that epoch, to make this list.

You can note that many items of this list have bacome elements of my final game (cannon, clock, paper, astronomy...). Some others were considered for some of my puzzles but didn't get implemented (mirror, soap...).

Here's the game ideas I scribbled afterwards, the "escape game" is at the very end. I wish I had decided to start with that immediately.



Days 1-2

I spent the first two days prototyping and thinking pretty thoroughly a "castles & cannonsé game concept where you successively play the role of a king who builds his castle, and his successor who needs to tumble it down with cannon balls. Each king has a successor that is a few dozen meters away, and needs to build his own castle from the bricks he removed from the previous king's castle, while having to build his own castle on a more and more inconvenient ground (slope, hill, mointain, ice, water, etc). The game would end when you run out of bricks.

The only problem is that besides the cannon firing, this was not fun at all, and I had to take the difficult decision to stop and restart from scratch with something else...

Days 3-4

The decision to make a castle escape game was taken on day 3... though, once again, I had no idea if I'd be able to do an interesting game. But I decided to try my best anyway. The two days were spent drawing a 16-room castle on paper and imagining all sorts of puzzles and interactions that could happen in it in no particular order.

Here are some HD photos:







All the elements and ideas of the final game are here, minus some adjustments (rooms inversions) and some ideas I had to abandon to release on time (using mirrors to reflect light, or seed plus bathroom water plus time to grow a plant, or a music room where you'd have to play some kind of Simon game to solve a puzzle). Fortunately, I think I made the good decisions, as the puzzles and events present in the final game are quite well balanced and sequenced.
